Listening to audiobooks, you must know some very good narrators. Some just make characters come alive; you might have also listened to narrators giving different voices to characters. Today, I am going to tell you about one such option: full-cast audiobooks.

You might have heard [<u>audio dramas</u>](https://www.bbc.co.uk/sounds/category/drama) by old radio stations; BBC used to do them a lot, with music and various casts. But sometimes they can be short and very abridged.

<br />

There are also 'full-cast audiobooks,' which can be unabridged or abridged. But abridged version do cover the entire book, dropping a few lines with/for sound effects and dropping stuff like he/she said, because their are multiple voice actors.  
Some of the popular ones on Audible:

- [Dune](https://amzn.to/4dQvfX3)
- [His Dark Materials](https://amzn.to/3NpYmGg)
- [American Gods](https://amzn.to/4eGNpvU)
- [The Sandman](https://amzn.to/4heXsdk)

<br />

## GraphicAudio

And there is one such studio that only produces dramatized adaptations of audiobooks - [<u>GraphicAudio</u>](https://www.graphicaudiointernational.net/). You can tell from their tagline, "A Movie in Your Mind," what they are about.  
You get many voice actors and sound effects like movies. Now, don't get confused by the word "movie." They can be unabridged or abridged, and just like other full-cast audiobooks, you almost get the whole book even in the abridged version.

If you are a fantasy fan, you will get a treat with their vast number of books under: fantasy, sci-fi, urban fantasy, fantasy romance, action/adventure, western, and [<u>other genres</u>](https://www.graphicaudiointernational.net/our-productions/genres.html).  
They also have some comic book adaptations like [Archie's](https://amzn.to/4dKQKZz), [The Boys](https://amzn.to/3U9NY97), and [Hellboy](https://amzn.to/3Y4YZdd).
